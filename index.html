Hi! I'm Raja...

I'm a Research Fellow / Pre-doctoral Researcher in the AI-infrastructure team at Microsoft Research India Lab (MSR-I), advised by Nipun Kwatra and Ramchandran Ramjee. I graduated from the Undergraduate Programmes at the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay in 2023, where I earned my B.Tech (with Honors) in Computer Science. My primary interests lie in Systems for ML, Compute Express Link (CXL), Networking, and Systems in general.

At MSR-I, I'm currently working on the compute-communication overlap of the transformer-based models (i.e., LLMs) in a multi-GPU setup. Additionally, in a very limited capacity, I'm also exploring memory management of CXL-based tiered memory systems using DAMON. During my undergrad, I worked with Prof. Purushottam Kulkarni on CXL and persistent memory.

I've also been curious about other sides of the classroom and worked as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant at the Dept. of Computer Science & Engineering, IIT Bombay, for more than a year. More specifically, I was a TA for Computer Networks Theory + Lab with Prof. Bhaskar Raman, Operating Systems Theory + Lab with Prof. Purushottam Kulkarni and Prof. Umesh Bellur, and Computer Systems Bootcamp with Prof. Mythili Vutukuru and Prof. Purushottam Kulkarni.

In the moments when time slows, you may find me taking an infinite walk or reading a book (mostly Hindi or Urdu poetry). Sometimes, as the sun sets in the western sky, I steal a few moments from the treasure of time and try to weave thoughts into words (check the poetry below!). I'm no great poet now, but someday in the future, I hope to be a decent one.
